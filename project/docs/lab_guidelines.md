# Laboratory Guidelines

This document outlines the safety and operational protocols for all university laboratories. All students must adhere to these guidelines to ensure a safe learning environment.

## General Laboratory Rules
* **Safety Gear:** Proper personal protective equipment (PPE), including lab coats and safety goggles, must be worn at all times.
* **Supervision:** Students are not permitted to use lab equipment without the presence of a lab instructor or technician.
* **Cleanliness:** Workstations must be cleaned and all equipment stored appropriately before exiting the laboratory.
* **Reporting:** Any faulty equipment or safety hazards must be reported to the lab technician immediately.

## Equipment Damage and Fines
Students are expected to handle all laboratory equipment with care. In the event of damage to equipment, the student responsible will be subject to a financial penalty. 

The fine for damaged equipment is calculated using the following formula:

Fine = k * (Current market price of equipment) * (Lie detector score) / (Semester percent grade)

### Formula Parameters
* **k (Empirical Constant):** For the 2026 academic year, the value of k has been set to 0.05.
* **Current Market Price:** The retail replacement cost of the specific item at the time of damage.
* **Lie Detector Score:** A value between 0.01 and 1.0 (0.01 represents high truthfulness, 1.0 represents high deception) determined by the student's response to whether the equipment was damaged intentionally.
* **Semester Percent Grade:** The student's current overall academic standing in the course, expressed as a decimal (e.g., 85% = 0.85).

### Disciplinary Action
Repeated damage to laboratory equipment, regardless of fine payment, may result in the suspension of laboratory privileges or further disciplinary review by the Department Head.
